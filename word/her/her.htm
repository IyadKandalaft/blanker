If you're not familiar with the style of joke format it goes something like this. The setup is usually Alice and Bob are dating and Bob's friend Chis comes to hang out with them and meet Alice for the first time.

Chris says 'What should we do?'
Bob, a gambler, replies 'Poker.'
Chris jokes 'Poke her? I barely know her!'

Below is a list of words like poker or liquor that can be misinterpreted easily to amuse/annoy your friends. The list is sorted into four sublists, the obviously dirty ones, ones that are dirty/funny if you say them right, ones that would work in the right situation and for completions sake ones that don't work at all.

Click Here for explanation

It was actually pretty easy. CMU publishes a dictionary of of words and how to pronounce them. "Her" actually has 2 different pronunciations depending on if you put a stress on the "er" sound. I went through this dictionary and got a list of words that also ended with the "er" sound.

There were about 8,300 words in that list. I then removed the ones that weren't words when you removed the "er" sound. For example "downriver" would be part of the 8,300 but "downriv" isn't a word. Removing words like "downriver" left about 6,500 words.

For the joke to work you need a verb like "poke" or "lick." So I removed all the words from the list that didn't have a verb form. This left around 1900 words.

The last thing I had to do was check that all of the words against a second dictionary because some of the words in CMU's dictionary are either obscure or foreign. I removed all the words that weren't also in the Wordnet dictionary. I was left with 1441 word pairs.

Then I just had to go through them and put them in the different categories. This took about 3 hours, or as I like to think about it enough time to watch Christmas movies such as Die Hard and Reindeer Games.
